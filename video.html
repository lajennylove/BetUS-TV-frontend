Game Preview

This Sunday at Highmark Stadium, the New York Jets (4-5, third in the AFC East) take on the Buffalo Bills (5-5, second in the AFC East), who present a formidable test.

The Buffalo Bills, who now have a 5-5 record, have played well in spite of their recent defeats by Cincinnati and Denver. The Jets will put the capacity to recover to the test. Josh Allen, Buffalo’s starting quarterback, leads the team offensively with 2600 yards, 19 touchdowns, and 11 interceptions.

The squad is eighth in the NFL in both total yards and points per contest with 86.5, and Stefon Diggs, the wide receiver, with 868 reception yards and 7 touchdowns, has been a major contributor.

The Bills are 13th in the NFL in terms of rushing yards per contest with 116.5, and with James Cook leading the way with 615 yards. Buffalo is ranked 12th in passing yards surrendered with 215.40 yards per game, and 5th in points surrendered with 18.4 per match, in terms of defense.
